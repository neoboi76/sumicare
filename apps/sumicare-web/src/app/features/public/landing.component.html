The Experience

Hot, Cold, and Everything Between

You start at the herbal pools — five of them, each at a different temperature, each scented with something different. Jasmine is the gentlest. Ginseng wakes you up. Hinoki smells like a cedar forest after rain. The Robust pool is where the regulars head first, and you will know why thirty seconds in.

The Thermal Pools

Soak, cool down in the ice room, soak again. The cycle is the point. It loosens what office hours and Manila traffic tighten, and after a few rounds the steam sauna stops feeling severe and starts feeling necessary.

The Kiln & the Ice

Five kilns line the corridor on the second floor — ice room, amethyst, Himalayan salt, ochre, and pine tree. Each one is a small clay-brick room you crawl into and sit in for ten minutes. The walls hold the warmth long after you leave.

Signature Therapy · Solo or paired

A Few Pointers

Best Times to Visit

Best times to visit: weekday mornings, before noon on Saturdays. The spa fills up in the late afternoon as office hours end. For couples, ask about private massage rooms — the shared setup in the standard area is fine for those comfortable with it, but privacy is available at a premium.

What to Bring

What to bring: shampoo, conditioner, body wash, facial wash, lotion in a small shower caddy. The jjimjilbang uniform is provided. Bring cash — not all services accept cards. Try the shikye (Korean rice beverage) and the boiled eggs from the snack bar: both have earned their cult status among regulars.
